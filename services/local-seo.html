LOCAL SEO FAQS

Deep technical answers on how we engineer local dominance.

How does EEAT affect Local SEO for contractors?

EEAT stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. It is the core framework Google uses for evaluating content quality. If a homeowner is trusting you to fix a gas line or replace a roof, Google demands proof of your expertise. We structure your site with detailed author bios, project case studies, licensing badges, and technically accurate service content to fulfill these stringent EEAT requirements and secure top rankings.

What is a Content Silo and why is it necessary?

A content silo is a method of grouping related web pages together to establish keyword topical authority. Instead of cramming all your plumbing services onto one page, we build a master Plumbing hub. We link this hub with dedicated child pages for Emergency Drain Cleaning, Water Heater Repair, and Slab Leaks. This mechanical structure forces search crawlers to recognize your deep and comprehensive expertise in specific verticals.

How do backlinks impact my local rankings?

Backlinks act as digital votes of confidence. However, in Local SEO, relevance trumps volume. A thousand links from spammy foreign directories will penalize you. We focus on building high authority and extremely local link profiles through local sponsorships, industry associations, and niche contractor directories. This signals immense localized trust to the algorithm.

Why is my competitor ranking higher when their website looks worse?

Search engines cannot see beautiful design. They read code and authority signals. Your competitor likely has an older and more established domain with a stronger backlink profile, better technical schema, or a highly optimized Google Business Profile with greater review velocity. Our Squad Audits reverse engineer their success, identifying exactly which technical levers we need to pull to overtake their position.

What is NAP Consistency and why does it break Map Pack rankings?

NAP stands for Name, Address, and Phone Number. Google verifies the legitimacy of your business by checking your data across the entire internet. If your address is listed as Suite B on your website but Ste B on Facebook, this data fragmentation confuses the algorithm. This results in a loss of trust and a drop in Map Pack rankings. We deploy data aggregators to enforce total parity across the web.

Can you rank my business in a city where I lack a physical address?

Yes, but it requires an advanced service area optimization strategy. While Google heavily biases Map Pack rankings toward physical proximity, we can capture immense organic search volume in surrounding cities by building highly targeted geographic pages. These pages combine local keyword data, specific neighborhood project portfolios, and precise ServiceArea Schema to dominate organic listings outside your immediate zip code.
